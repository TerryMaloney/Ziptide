# ZIPTIDE — Story & Hooks (opening arc + the long game)

Creative direction for the opening and the plot hooks that make the world believable.
Story stays as set unless Terry says otherwise. This doc captures decisions made 2026-06-15.

---

## The core twist (the spine of the whole game)
The player naturally **assumes Cal is human from Earth**. She isn't. Cal is an alien contract
technician working the Ziptide containment network. **Earth is discovered near the END** — the
reveal recontextualizes everything: the "containment network" was built to keep humanity's
stories (and maybe humanity itself) sealed away. We never start on Earth; we *earn* Earth.

This means: **the opening world is alien** (Toxic Venice / W001), not Earth. Believability comes
from a lived-in, internally-consistent alien society — not from it looking like Earth.

## What makes the opening world believable (the "Halo" trick)
Halo sold its world through *implication* — environments, audio logs, and offhand dialogue that
imply a vast history you only partly see. Apply the same:
- **Visible class divide:** toxic lower canals where the workers/Cal operate, gleaming oligarch
  glass towers above. You feel the society without exposition.
- **Tool-built infrastructure:** Cal is a repair tech; rusted walkways, patched conduits, jury-rigged
  signal nodes tell you people live and work here.
- **Environmental readables / glyphs:** alien oligarch glyphs on the towers, warning decals on the
  toxic canals — language you can't read but clearly *means* something.
- **Offhand RILL lines:** RILL reacting to the world ("This district was sealed before I went
  dark.") implies deep time and history.
- **One mystery object per world:** something that doesn't fit, that pays off later (a sealed door,
  a node that resists repair). W001's: the signal node behaves oddly when repaired — first hint the
  network isn't what Cal was told.

## Early plot seeds (plant now, pay off later)
- **Cal's non-humanity:** subtle — her reflection, her hands, how NPCs/RILL address her species.
  Don't state it; let it sit wrong until the Earth reveal.
- **The containment hook (W001→W004):** Cal thinks she's repairing infrastructure. RILL wakes and,
  by W004, asks *"What are you carrying that requires containment?"* — the thread that unravels the
  whole "this is a prison, not a utility" truth in Chapter 2.
- **The Wake Guild** (who reprogrammed RILL 3 years ago) is the faction that hired/handles Cal —
  trust them at first, doubt them later.

## RILL in the opening (companion = your emotional anchor)
- **W001 boot:** "Systems nominal. I think." (dry, glitchy, not yet a person)
- RILL is **Dormant→Stirring** through W001–W004: short functional lines, occasional unprompted
  observation that hints at memory. RILL is the player's guide and the game's conscience.

## The opening emotional arc (what the player should FEEL)
1. **Competent but small** — you're a worker in someone else's world.
2. **Curious** — something's slightly off about the job.
3. **Capable** — you can move, shoot, and (teaser) *fly*.
4. **Hooked** — RILL's question makes you want the next world.

See `LEVEL1_TOXIC_VENICE.md` for how these map onto the first level's beats.
