# Optical Illusions in VR — parked design note (explore later)

**Status: PARKED idea (Terry, 2026-06-21).** Not scheduled — capture so it's not lost. Fits naturally
into the **Pattern** worlds + the **contained-universe meta** (`storyboard/STORY_BIBLE.md`): illusions =
the simulation's seams showing. VR is the rare medium where these actually work (real depth + the player
physically moves their head).

## Why it fits Ziptide (not a gimmick — it's the theme)
- **The Pattern is the universe's geometry waking up** → impossible geometry / shifting space is its
  native visual language.
- **The meta = "this world is a constructed, observed system."** An **anamorphic** world (only coherent
  from one viewpoint) literally *is* "real only from where the Observers watch" — the late-game reveal as
  a mechanic.
- Already seeded: **W006 Mirror Flats** (reflection lags ½ second), **W015 The Soft Geometry**,
  W021 Lattice, W029 Pattern Shore, W039 Pattern City, W054 Broken Pattern.

## The VR illusion toolbox
- **Anamorphic reveals** — scattered fragments that align into a real object/door/glyph **only from one
  exact spot**; you move your head to "solve" it. (Pairs with the alien-glyph mystery thread.)
- **Impossible geometry** — loop corridors (walk straight, return to start), rooms bigger inside than out,
  Escher stairs, portal-stitched spaces (render-texture portals).
- **Forced-perspective scale** (Superliminal-style) — an object's real size = how big it looks; grab a
  "tiny" far rock → it's a boulder up close. Could be a real tool (great with the gravity glove).
- **Illusion-camo enemies** — a drone invisible while still, visible only when moving (or vice-versa) →
  the **wrist scanner is the counter** (cuts through the illusion to show what's real). Makes illusions a
  *readable* mechanic, not just frustration.
- **Mirror tricks** — extend W006: mirrors that are doorways, or reflect a different room / a you that
  acts on its own.
- **Color/afterimage** — stare at a color, see its complement to reveal a hidden path. Ties to **RILL's
  color (W024)** — the named color could be the "key" only visible as an afterimage.

## Mechanics it could power
Puzzles (align the viewpoint; afterimage reveals) · traversal (impossible-stairs shortcuts, TARDIS rooms)
· stealth/combat (illusion-camo enemies, fake vs real walls) · the **big reveal** (a whole world exposed
as a flat, seamed anamorphic construction once you step off the intended path).

## ⚠️ Comfort guardrails (VR — important)
Favor **visual/spatial** illusions over **vestibular** ones. Non-euclidean *walking* and forced motion
cause nausea fast. Keep it: slow, optional, **no forced camera moves**, "solve by moving your own head."
The head-movement-anamorphic kind is both the safest and the most magical. (Honors the all-ages comfort
charter in `STORY_BIBLE.md` §9.)

## If/when we build it
Start tiny: one **anamorphic door puzzle** in a Pattern world (align fragments from a marked spot) + the
**scanner-reveals-the-real-geometry** pairing. Pure visual/material + a viewpoint check — no new locomotion,
low risk. Distill into the relevant `storyboard/W0xx_*/README.md` when scheduled.
